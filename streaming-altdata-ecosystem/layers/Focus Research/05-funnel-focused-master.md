# Layer 5 Focused Research: Funnel (Downstream Intent and Discovery)

Layer 5 sits at the top of the consumer conversion funnel, capturing search navigation, watchlist additions, and app-store download velocity long before a billing relationship exists for the Wallet layer's transaction panels to record; because the Layer 0 gatekeepers refuse to break out standalone streaming metrics, its vendors supply the synthetic demand proxies from which analysts and advertising agencies reconstruct subscriber momentum ahead of disclosure. Prior passes tagged this layer the ecosystem's largest blind spot: vendors that present as consumer-facing utility applications while quietly operating large business-to-business enterprise data pipelines and programmatic media buying engines. This focused pass had two assignments beyond consolidation: confirm, via a named client, that Sensor Tower's primary client base is broad app-economy intelligence rather than streaming-exclusive, and surface a primary-source (non-estimator-site) revenue figure for JustWatch or Reelgood. Verdict up front: both gaps remain open. The source adds directional evidence on the first and nothing primary on the second; each verdict gets its own section below, at its actual evidentiary weight.

## Reverse-engineering customer acquisition cost pipelines

Customer acquisition cost efficiency is the primary metric dictating platform viability in the current streaming economy, and none of the platforms publish it: Amazon Prime Video, Apple TV+, and Netflix disclose nothing about paid marketing efficiency or their organic-versus-paid acquisition mix, so institutional analysts construct synthetic proxy frameworks ahead of quarterly earnings disclosures. The mobile-marketing backdrop makes the modeling harder. After Apple's App Tracking Transparency rollout, global opt-in rates plateaued at approximately 27%, gutting deterministic attribution models and forcing a reliance on probabilistic tracking. Average cost per install for iOS applications surged to $5.84 by 2026, a massive premium over the $1.92 Android average, and day-30 retention across all application categories averages just 5.4% (subscription-based entertainment apps do meaningfully better, at roughly 13.8% to 14%), leaving platforms straining to hold a lifetime-value-to-customer-acquisition-cost (LTV:CAC) ratio above the 3:1 benchmark the industry treats as sustainable.

The workaround is a triangulation methodology spanning three vendor positions inside the layer.

| Funnel stage | Measurement vendor | Core metric tracked | Function in CAC modeling |
|---|---|---|---|
| Top-of-funnel intent | JustWatch / Reelgood | Watchlist additions, search query volume | Establishes organic baseline demand prior to ad spend |
| Mid-funnel expenditure | Pathmatics (Sensor Tower) | Estimated ad spend, impression volume, creative rotation | Quantifies capital deployed to drive user acquisition |
| Bottom-funnel velocity | Sensor Tower / data.ai | App download estimates, store ranking momentum, review volume | Proxies gross subscriber additions and conversion |

The mechanics: JustWatch and Reelgood aggregate millions of consumer search queries and watchlist additions across global markets, so a tentpole series launch shows up as a search-navigation spike quantifying the pre-transactional organic demand generated by the intellectual property itself. Pathmatics (the digital marketing intelligence subsidiary integrated into Sensor Tower) estimates the digital ad spend, creative rotation, and impression volume the platform deploys across social media and display networks to capture that demand during the launch window, when platforms aggressively bid up mobile and connected-television inventory. Sensor Tower then supplies the conversion event: app-store ranking velocity, download estimates, and review volume across the iOS App Store and Google Play Store, with download velocity serving as the immediate proxy for subscriber additions. Analysts lag the JustWatch intent signal by a defined operational window to match the corresponding download surge, then divide estimated Pathmatics advertising spend by Sensor Tower net new download estimates, adjusting the denominator for the organic baseline. The interpretive rules follow directly. Heavy Pathmatics spend plus weak download velocity plus flat search intent means acquisition cost for that cohort is spiraling, signaling fundamental friction in the growth narrative. High intent plus surging downloads plus low measured spend means organic virality and efficient capital deployment, a leading indicator of margin expansion in the subsequent quarter.

## Statistical controls and the validation record

Raw correlation between a demand spike and a download surge cannot be treated as causal, and the research is explicit about the two confounds that must be actively controlled. First, concurrent pricing architecture changes: a promotional cross-sell discount or subsidized bundled tier launched alongside a tentpole release (the Disney+/Hulu/Max bundle architecture is the standing example) means download velocity cannot be attributed to marketing efficiency alone, and the model must also mark down the suppressed lifetime value of the discounted cohort. Second, competitive interference: a rival platform releasing competing premium content on the same weekend suppresses organic search intent and inflates required bidding costs on mobile ad networks simultaneously.

The validation record carries two dated anchors. The defining historical case is the Q2 2022 Netflix subscriber contraction: institutional analysts on Sensor Tower data identified the platform's lowest United States download quarter since Q4 2014, and that app-store velocity signal predicted the subscriber loss announcements that subsequently hit the equity valuation. More recently, in Q1 2026, JustWatch watchlist-intent data was used to predict that Disney+ (capturing 16% of intent) and Apple TV+ (12%) were narrowing the market share gap against incumbents Netflix (19%) and Prime Video (17%), a live demonstration that pre-transactional intent leads reported figures.

## JustWatch Media: the programmatic engine behind the consumer guide

The standing misread of this layer is that discovery aggregators are consumer utilities subsidized by affiliate routing fees. JustWatch's free streaming guide is better understood as the data-harvesting front end of a programmatic advertising business. Founded in Berlin in 2014 by CEO David Croyé, JustWatch processes the behavioral data of 50 to 60 million monthly active users across 139 to 140 global markets with a workforce of approximately 200 to 243 employees (the ranges reflect divergent secondary estimates in the source and are carried as ranges rather than reconciled). Every exact click-through action, search query, and watchlist addition feeds highly deterministic behavioral graphs, monetized through JustWatch Media, a dedicated programmatic media buying and data licensing arm built exclusively for the entertainment industry. Estimated annual revenue runs $50 million to $100 million, heavily eclipsing any consumer-oriented affiliate revenue stream; note that this is an estimate, not a disclosed figure (see the revenue-gap verdict below).

The targeting model is the differentiator. Demographic targeting is notoriously inefficient in entertainment (genre preferences cross age, gender, and geographic boundaries; the person who fits the romantic-comedy demographic may fundamentally prefer psychological thrillers), so JustWatch rejects probabilistic demographic models and builds deterministic "taste profiles" instead. Users opt into data collection inside the guide; every interaction (every search, every click-out to a streaming provider, every watchlist addition) lands in a centralized in-house data management platform; and the machine learning recommendation algorithms originally developed over a decade for the consumer app's discovery features map precise content affinities. The output is non-obvious segments, such as a cohort that consistently streams science fiction and dark comedy, completely agnostic of age or gender identifiers, activated for campaign deployment when a major studio like Universal, Paramount, or Sony launches a theatrical release or streaming series matching those parameters.

Execution is vertically integrated end to end. JustWatch engineered its own proprietary demand-side platform (DSP), specifically calibrated to ingest the first-party taste profiles and deploy targeted media buys across YouTube, Meta, TikTok, and connected-television networks, deliberately bypassing standard open-web programmatic exchanges (rampant display ad fraud, low-quality placements, technical fragmentation) in favor of high-engagement, digital-first video channels where entertainment discovery actually occurs. Proprietary bidding algorithms built on historical machine learning models serve trailer creative to the audiences with the highest mathematical probability of engagement, and the stack explicitly integrates with the B2B intent platforms 6sense and Demandbase to align downstream consumer intent with broader enterprise marketing initiatives.

The closed loop is the value proposition: measured deterministic intent rather than viewability or click-through rates. JustWatch surveys the specific audiences built for campaigns as early as three to six months before a title's release to establish baseline awareness and intent, then cross-references campaign exposure data with downstream actions inside the consumer application (the advertised movie added to a watchlist, an outbound click to purchase a cinema ticket). If survey data shows an audience segment at 78% intent to watch a film in theaters, the bidding algorithm aggressively scales expenditure against that cohort; capital automatically shifts away from segments showing passive awareness but low intent to transact.

## Reelgood: the metadata barcode problem and the quant API

Reelgood runs the same consumer-guide-in-front, enterprise-data-behind architecture from San Francisco. Founded in 2015 by CEO David Sanderson, it has raised approximately $13.3 million across three funding rounds, including a $6.8 million Series A in December 2019 led by Runa Capital, and operates on a headcount of 27 to 43 with an estimated annual recurring revenue of roughly $8.4 million (an estimator-site figure; see the revenue-gap verdict below). It provides B2B analytics and competitive intelligence for entertainment clients, but its strategic weight in this framework comes from deep penetration into quantitative finance: multi-strategy hedge funds, private equity analysts, and corporate development teams license the Reelgood Partner API to feed raw, machine-readable catalog telemetry directly into algorithmic trading models and financial forecasting engines. The underlying asset is normalized streaming availability across more than 300 platforms globally, over 4 million individual titles, 25 countries, and more than 7 years of deep historical windowing data.

What Reelgood actually sells the financial sector is not list aggregation; it is the programmatic resolution of the entertainment industry's most persistent data engineering failure, the absence of a universal content identifier. Publishing has the ISBN; streaming has divergent naming conventions, regional title variants, and disparate database architectures on every platform. The Reelgood Matching AI, trained over eight years on the behavioral feedback of more than 100 million consumer app users, continuously ingests over 55 million raw data entities from the 300-plus providers and runs a four-stage pipeline without manual intervention: ingestion (raw, un-normalized entities arriving under each service's own identifiers), normalization (extracting and standardizing cast, crew, runtime, synopsis, release date, and production studio details), machine learning resolution (confidence scoring across all known entities, deterministically merging records when the normalized signals align, which resolves director's cuts, remakes, and regional variants without any shared external ID), and canonical output (a single canonical ID per verified record). The pipeline reduces the 55 million raw signals to 4.2 million verified, deduplicated master titles, delivered to enterprise clients via REST API or bulk AWS S3 export within five minutes of initial ingestion.

| API specification | Figure (per Reelgood's business-facing product documentation) |
|---|---|
| Data delivery format | JSON over HTTPS (TLS 1.2+), bulk AWS S3 export |
| Ingestion-to-output latency | Under 5 minutes |
| Throughput capacity | 50 calls per second limit |
| Reliability guarantee | 99.9% monthly API uptime SLA |
| Catalog scale tracked | 4.2 million unique titles across 300+ platforms |
| Historical data depth | 7+ years of timestamped availability windows |

The financial applications are concrete. Because the gatekeepers strictly withhold internal engagement metrics, catalog depth and historical availability windows serve as the most accurate external proxies for platform health and subscriber retention. Funds monitor the exact real-time composition of competitor catalogs through deduplicated counts (Netflix at 4,404 distinct titles versus HBO Max at 2,168 and Paramount+ at 881), track "free agent" titles transitioning between platforms, and cut catalogs by genre for gap analysis (2,614 independent drama titles versus 1,074 comedy titles). Pay-One window tracking is the signature use case: Universal's The Super Mario Bros. Movie followed a strict 18-month Pay-One rotation after its theatrical run, debuting on Peacock in August 2023, moving to Netflix in December 2023, returning to Peacock in October 2024, shifting to Prime Video in February 2025, and cycling back to Peacock in February 2026. When historical tracking shows a major catalog asset scheduled to depart a platform, quantitative models immediately project a churn-velocity spike for that service in the subsequent month; when the API flags a highly anticipated arrival (Alien: Romulus, Anora), models forecast an immediate reduction in customer acquisition costs and an engagement surge for the quarter. With the API integrated directly into trading infrastructure, algorithmic funds actively trade the delta between catalog-indicated platform health and Wall Street consensus estimates ahead of quarterly earnings announcements.

## Sensor Tower after data.ai: monopoly economics

Bottom-of-funnel conversion measurement was, for over a decade, a fierce duopoly between Sensor Tower and data.ai (formerly App Annie), both operating massive opt-in consumer panels and machine learning models estimating mobile downloads, daily active user engagement, and in-app purchase revenue across the iOS App Store and Google Play ecosystems. That duopoly was permanently dismantled in March 2024, when Sensor Tower executed a surprise acquisition of data.ai for an undisclosed sum, a deal that shocked the industry because data.ai (which had previously raised $157 million and employed around 400 staff) was widely considered the larger of the two. Bain Capital Credit led the financing, supported by follow-on capital from existing backer Riverwood Capital (which had injected $45 million into Sensor Tower in May 2020) and a new strategic investment from Paramark Ventures (Shinhan Securities' strategic investment was a separate, later event, announced October 2025 per Sensor Tower's own press release, not part of the data.ai financing).

The integration followed the private equity playbook without apology. Management under CEO Oliver Yeh laid off approximately 200 legacy data.ai employees (roughly half of data.ai's pre-merger staff), with the reductions concentrated in sales, operations, and account management, signaling an institutional mandate to extract margin synergies, consolidate redundant data science pipelines, and drive the unified entity toward maximum profitability. The combined company services over 2,000 enterprise customers worldwide, described in the source as including major tech conglomerates and financial institutions, and holds what the research characterizes as a near-monopoly over mobile app intelligence, with Apptopia the sole remaining mid-market alternative. Executives positioned the merger as bolstering data quality, accuracy, and differential privacy compliance across geographies; institutional buyers experienced it as the end of negotiating leverage.

Pre-merger, quantitative funds and marketing agencies routinely played the two vendors against each other for contract terms. Post-merger, Sensor Tower operates with absolute pricing power: strict annual contracts, no self-serve signups, no public pricing pages, no free trials. Pricing is structured around discrete modules (Store Intelligence, Advertising Intelligence, Usage Intelligence) multiplied by seat counts, app-category coverage, and geographic market parameters. Per anonymized transaction telemetry from B2B software procurement databases (Vendr is the cited source), the median Sensor Tower deployment currently runs approximately $74,415 per year.

| Institutional buyer segment | Module and coverage scope | Estimated annual cost |
|---|---|---|
| Small studio / boutique | One or two modules, limited regional coverage, three to five seats | $30,000 to $70,000 |
| Mid-market / agency | Two modules, broad regional coverage, five to fifteen seats | $70,000 to $150,000 |
| Enterprise / hedge fund | All modules, global coverage, fifteen or more seats, full API access | $150,000 to well over $300,000 |
| API integration toll | Programmatic rate limits, S3/Snowflake delivery add-ons | Additional $5,000 to $20,000 |

Programmatic access is separately toll-gated: the Sensor Tower Connect API carries incremental licensing fees of $5,000 to $20,000 annually depending on requested rate limits and computational usage caps, with standard concurrency restricted to 500 requests per minute per contract and expensive upgrades required for heavy resource-consuming endpoints.

The lock-in mechanism is the deeper story. The Connect suite migrates institutional clients off basic API polling and into embedded data infrastructure: for Snowflake shops, secure data sharing drops app performance, usage metrics, and consumer insights directly into the client's warehouse, refreshed daily and explorable by country and device type with no custom extraction scripts; alternatively, the Scheduled Data Feed delivers bulk historical metrics as optimized Parquet or CSV files into an Amazon S3 bucket or preferred cloud store. These integrations eliminate REST latency and rate-limit friction, and they function as the ultimate renewal guarantee: once a multi-strategy fund hard-codes a Sensor Tower Snowflake share into the automated algorithms forecasting Netflix's ad-tier growth or Apple's mobile acquisition costs, ripping out that pipeline and migrating to a challenger becomes economically prohibitive. The recurring revenue that results is the return thesis for the private equity syndicate that financed the data.ai acquisition.

## The existential threat: agentic discovery

The entire layer's economics rest on one premise: consumers actively navigate the open web or dedicated aggregator applications to find content, generating the digital exhaust (search queries, clicks, watchlist additions) that gets normalized, packaged, and monetized as B2B intelligence and taste profiles. Conversational AI attacks that premise directly. As consumer behavior migrates toward large language models and agentic assistants as primary discovery engines, the top-of-funnel signal goes completely dark: when a user asks an agent to find a highly rated thriller available on their current subscriptions and play it, the agent queries inventory and executes the transaction autonomously, and the intent signal is captured entirely inside the foundation model's closed ecosystem, where legacy aggregators cannot scrape, record, or monetize the interaction. The shift is already visible in four places the source names: Tubi's native ChatGPT integration, Netflix's internal generative-AI voice-search defenses, the Bill Gates-backed startup Likewise launching "Pix" (an SMS, email, and CTV-native conversational recommendation agent trained on 600 million data points), and Lumio's "Project Neo" (a WhatsApp and Instagram-native agentic remote control).

The survival pivot is to become the ground truth for the models. Rather than relying on consumers to visit proprietary applications, vendors like Reelgood are repositioning as the foundational data layer grounding the LLMs themselves, supplying canonical availability APIs directly to AI developers so that generated responses reflect accurate, real-time streaming options, with freshness-aware pipelines and tool calls reducing model hallucination. Securing those enterprise API contracts ensures short-term survival, but it destroys the lucrative programmatic advertising model: if the consumer interacts solely with an AI assistant, JustWatch Media can no longer capture deterministic taste profiles, build proprietary audiences, or serve optimized video advertisements, because the interface belongs entirely to the AI provider. The research's conclusion is blunt and worth carrying in full: the transition from ad-supported consumer interface to commoditized backend LLM data supplier is a permanent margin compression event for the entire Downstream Intent layer.

## What this layer means for the framework

Layer 5 is the predictive mechanism of the stack: triangulating JustWatch search intent against Pathmatics ad spend and Sensor Tower download velocity is the closest thing institutional analysts have to piercing gatekeeper opacity ahead of disclosure, and the method has two dated validations (Q2 2022 Netflix, the Q1 2026 intent-share call) on the board. The audit beneath that headline shows an ecosystem defined less by consumer utility than by consolidation and B2B monetization: JustWatch running a closed-loop DSP on deterministic taste profiles, Reelgood selling the industry's missing barcode to quantitative funds, and a private-equity-backed Sensor Tower monopoly imposing enterprise pricing floors and API tolls on everyone downstream. Both gaps this pass targeted stay open. And the layer's own foundation, digital exhaust from human navigation, is precisely what agentic AI is draining: vendors that re-architect as LLM ground truth survive with compressed margins, and vendors that do not lose the signal entirely. Outputs here feed the Wallet layer's conversion confirmations and the Demand/Valuation layer's forecasting models elsewhere in the tree.
