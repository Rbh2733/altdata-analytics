# Layer 5: Funnel (Downstream Intent & Discovery)

The Funnel layer sits at the top of the streaming conversion funnel, upstream of every transaction. It tracks the behavioral precursors to subscriber acquisition: search navigation, watchlist additions, application-store download velocity, and platform click-through rates, all captured before any billing relationship exists. Its analytical value peaks during tentpole-title launch windows, when platforms run aggressive performance-marketing bursts and bid up mobile and connected-TV advertising inventory to drive app downloads; by tracking upstream intent against that ad spend, analysts can reverse-engineer a platform's customer acquisition cost efficiency well before quarterly disclosure. The layer has historically been the least documented segment of the streaming alt-data map, largely because its vendors present as consumer utilities while their actual enterprise architectures are B2B data licensing and advertising engines. The findings below draw on two Gemini Deep Research passes, a Pro pass and a Flash pass added 2026-07-08, that turned out to be complementary rather than competing across nearly every question this layer was asked to answer.

## Ownership and scale: three very different companies wearing similar consumer apps

JustWatch GmbH was founded August 14, 2014 in Berlin by David Croyé, Christoph Hoyer, Kevin Hiller, Dominik Raute, Ingke Purrmann, and Michael Wilken. Croyé had been Chief Marketing Officer at kaufDA, sold to Axel Springer for roughly $40 million in March 2011, and recycled personal proceeds from that exit into JustWatch's initial funding. The initial bootstrap totaled roughly $2.5 million, about $500,000 of founder capital plus roughly $2 million in public innovation grants and private loans; the company launched in the US and Germany in February 2015 and reached profitability within its first year. External capital has been deliberately minimal: in 2017, after breakeven, JustWatch took an undisclosed seed round led by Cologne-based STS Ventures, built on Stephan Schubert's "camel" capital-efficiency philosophy of surviving funding winters on high unit contribution margins with no vanity metrics, alongside Christian Gaiser and a consortium of roughly eight angel investors, with the founders retaining majority voting control. In December 2018 the company received a €223,544 (about $253,000) EU grant, structured as prize money, allocated to database infrastructure and the visibility of European film and television works. In December 2019 JustWatch used internal cash flow to acquire its New York rival GoWatchIt from Plexus Entertainment, absorbing a competitor and establishing a North American operational beachhead without dilutive financing.

By the 2024-2026 window JustWatch had grown past 40 million monthly active users by late 2023 and past 50 million monthly streamers (estimates run as high as 60 million) across 139-140 countries, with its backend indexing over 470,000 film and TV titles across more than 1,100 streaming services. Headcount grew from 56 employees in 2020 to a range of 200-243 by 2026, spanning 46 nationalities across a distributed office network covering Berlin, Los Angeles, New York, London, Paris, Rome, Madrid, Munich, and Dubai. Revenue sits at a 2026 point estimate of roughly $68 million within a credible $50 million to $100 million range built from recruitment telemetry and specialized financial intelligence platforms; the company is private with no public filings, so all revenue figures are analyst estimates, and the higher end of the range is consistent with the scale of its programmatic media buying arm, which manages entertainment advertising budgets across more than 100 markets.

Reelgood was founded in 2015 in San Francisco by David Sanderson, built to solve streaming fragmentation with a unified cross-service search engine and guide. Total capital raised runs $13.3 million to $15.4 million depending on whether a debt facility is counted: a $1 million seed in April 2015 led by Harrison Metal, a $3 million extended seed in November 2016 with 8VC, Accel, Acrew Capital, Lerer Hippeau, and Slow Ventures, a $3.5 million venture round in July 2017 led by August Capital with Harrison Metal and Social Capital, a $6.75-6.8 million Series A in December 2019 led by Runa Capital with August Capital continuing, and a $4.1 million structured debt facility in March 2022 that extended runway without further equity dilution. In October 2018 Reelgood acquired rival metadata supplier Guidebox, internalizing its data supply chain after concluding that legacy metadata providers, including Nielsen's Gracenote, delivered only 60-70% accuracy on real-time streaming availability. The consumer app's more than 100 million users (Reelgood's own technology page states its matching engine trained on feedback from over 100 million consumer app users; data.reelgood.com/technology/) supply behavioral training data for its proprietary AI-driven metadata matching engine, the company's primary enterprise asset, tracking over 4 million individual titles across more than 300 streaming services in 25-plus international markets. Reelgood runs lean, with headcount estimates spanning 27 to 43 employees through 2024-2026 and revenue estimates of $5.8 million to $11.1 million annually (annual recurring revenue pegged around $8.4 million, revenue per employee around $196,000). Its strategic weight exceeds its revenue: enterprise clients across hardware, search, and finance depend structurally on its availability API.

Sensor Tower was founded in 2013 in San Francisco by Oliver Yeh and Alex Malafeev, incubated through AngelPad, with a $1 million seed round in August 2013 led by Rembrandt Venture Partners, independently confirmed against AngelPad's own blog during this review. The founders ran a profit-first, bootstrapped model for the first five to seven years, retaining over 70% voting control through year five with minimal dilution, until Riverwood Capital injected $45 million of Series B growth equity in May 2020, converting a founder-controlled startup into an institutionally backed scale-up. By 2023 the cap table spanned founders, Riverwood, and an expanded employee stock ownership plan, and the transformation completed in March 2024 with the acquisition of primary rival data.ai, formerly App Annie, which had raised $157 million in venture capital, for an undisclosed sum, financed by a debt-and-equity syndicate led by Bain Capital Credit with follow-on participation from Riverwood Capital and Paramark Ventures. Governance shifted decisively to private equity stewardship, with Riverwood's Francisco Alvarez-Demalde chairing the board and directing M&A strategy and Bain Capital Credit holding observer seats and protective provisions over liquidity events, IPOs, and enterprise sales; post-merger integration included a targeted reduction of roughly 200 legacy data.ai staff, mostly in redundant sales, operations, and account management. Consolidation continued with 2025 acquisitions of Video Game Insights and Playliner extending coverage into PC and console gaming, and in October 2025 South Korea's Shinhan Securities made a strategic investment earmarked for generative-AI data enhancements and the PC and console expansion. As of 2025/2026, Sensor Tower tracks over 20 million applications and trillions of data points across an application economy valued above $130 billion, with consolidated annual recurring revenue estimated in the $200 million to $250 million-plus range and an estimated 45%-plus share of the premium mobile intelligence market; the board is reportedly optimizing EBITDA margins toward a potential IPO in late 2026 or 2027.

## Two genuinely different business models hiding behind similar consumer apps

Neither JustWatch nor Reelgood monetizes primarily through consumer-facing affiliate or referral fees, and neither has executed a business model shift since 2024; both were already running sophisticated B2B models before then, and it is market understanding that caught up rather than the models that changed.

JustWatch is, functionally, a programmatic media buying business wearing a consumer app. Roughly 80% of operational focus and the large majority of revenue flows through its B2B division, JustWatch Media. The consumer application functions as a first-party data harvesting mechanism: every search, age-rating filter, watchlist addition, and click-through to playback feeds deterministic audience profiles, roughly 100 million amassed to date. JustWatch Media uses those profiles to run targeted campaigns across YouTube, Meta, TikTok, and connected-TV environments on behalf of the entertainment industry exclusively, over 20,000 campaigns for more than 100 enterprise clients including Universal Pictures, Paramount Pictures, Sony Pictures, 20th Century Studios, and Amazon Prime Video. Pricing is a high-margin cost-per-engagement model rather than industry-standard cost-per-mille, optimized against measured downstream intent, box office conversion, verified SVOD sign-ups, rather than impression volume, using deterministic taste clusters rather than probabilistic demographics. A further strategic extension landed in January 2026 when JustWatch hired Quentin Carbonell, previously director of acquisitions at Mubi and then head of streaming campaign and content marketing planning for HBO Max in EMEA, as Senior Vice President of Global Content Acquisitions and Strategy, its first move into direct content licensing, using its demand data to acquire digital licenses for scripted and unscripted content and distribute directly to its user base.

Reelgood, by contrast, is an enterprise metadata licensing business. Revenue derives from syndicating normalized, real-time streaming availability data via premium APIs through Reelgood for Business, plus the analytics arm Reelgood Insights, led by Mark Green, formerly Nielsen's senior vice president of global measurement. The client base spans technology hardware manufacturers (Roku, Microsoft), smart TV ecosystems, search engines, voice assistant developers, streaming platforms (Philo, Plex), unified guide platforms (FreeCast), and financial-sector buyers. The differentiating asset is historical depth: over seven years of timestamped availability records enable "Distribution Arc" analysis of how titles migrate from paid SVOD to free ad-supported platforms. From 1.93 million availability windows across 240 platforms, Reelgood documented the median paid-to-free window collapsing from 6 months for 2020 releases to 2.5 months for 2025 releases, with wide studio dispersion: Disney holds titles off free platforms a median 46 months to protect its SVOD ecosystem, while Sony/Columbia sequences to free in 13 months. Post-2024 extensions include "Content Calculus," supporting M&A due diligence with title-by-title library overlap analysis and competitive benchmarking for consolidation scenarios such as the Warner Bros. Discovery outcome; FAST-ecosystem metadata and electronic programming guide mapping (the FAST market generated $4.1 billion in 2023 revenue across roughly 216 million monthly users and 1,400-plus channels from 22 providers); and LLM grounding, marketing its 99%-accurate real-time availability API to AI developers as the ground-truth layer that prevents chatbots from hallucinating where a title streams.

The two models are cleanly distinct: JustWatch monetizes the consumer audience by delivering programmatic advertising for studios, while Reelgood monetizes the metadata itself through enterprise licensing, API syndication, and strategic analytics.

## Sensor Tower is a general app-analytics company that streaming analysts borrow, not a streaming vendor

Sensor Tower is a general app-analytics company that streaming analysts borrow from, and the distinction from the Wallet layer is categorical. Wallet-layer vendors such as YipitData and Consumer Edge work from realized financial transactions, credit and debit card exhaust and bank API feeds isolating actual billing relationships, cohort survival curves, and plan-mix migration. Sensor Tower operates pre-transaction, estimating download velocity, in-app revenue, engagement, and advertising spend from anonymized panel and machine-learning models across the whole $130 billion-plus app economy, with a product matrix and client base that are cross-sector by design: mobile gaming publishers, retail and e-commerce, consumer packaged goods conglomerates such as Procter & Gamble, financial services firms such as Western Union, and hedge funds as alt-data consumers.

Streaming analysts have co-opted three specific mechanisms from that broader toolkit. Store Intelligence treats app-store velocity as a gross-add proxy, since a download is a prerequisite for mobile subscription and download spikes coinciding with tentpole releases proxy new-subscriber momentum in near real time; Sensor Tower's post-IDFA probabilistic models produce download estimates routinely within roughly 5% of officially reported figures, with some framings putting the margin at 5-10% against reported financials. Usage Intelligence treats "Power User" engagement intensity as a churn proxy, since the distribution of days-per-month an app is opened tracks platform stickiness and declining session frequency reliably leads reported cancellation upticks. And Pathmatics supplies ad intelligence for CAC modeling, full-funnel visibility into a platform's digital ad spend, creatives, and impressions across display, social, video, and OTT, correlated against subsequent install spikes to model true customer acquisition cost efficiency. Sensor Tower is, in short, the infrastructural monitor of the global digital economy whose data exhaust happens to provide the highest-frequency telemetry available for streaming CAC and churn modeling; it does not position itself as a discovery portal and does not compete with JustWatch or Reelgood for consumers. One caveat carries over from both source passes: the claim that its client base is broad app-economy intelligence rather than streaming-exclusive is asserted through product-vertical descriptions rather than confirmed via a named client engagement.

## AI-agent discovery is disintermediating the layer on multiple fronts at once

The disintermediation mechanism here is structural, not speculative. If a consumer asks an AI assistant "what should I watch" and the agent executes the search, applies preferences, and initiates playback natively, the traditional discovery portal never sees the visit, and therefore never harvests the first-party behavioral data that funds its B2B model: no data point, no taste profile, no programmatic targeting engine for JustWatch, no tracked intent for enterprise syndication for Reelgood. The broader behavioral shift has been described as "Search Everywhere Optimization," discovery dissolving out of centralized portals into AI assistants, social platforms, and conversational interfaces, with agentic AI going beyond recommendations to verify a user's active subscriptions, cross-reference live catalogs via API, and start playback on the host device.

The named entrant and event landscape spans 2025 and 2026. Fox Corporation's free ad-supported platform Tubi launched a native application inside OpenAI's ChatGPT on April 7, 2026, the first major streaming service to integrate directly into a conversational AI interface; users prompt in natural language, the agent queries Tubi's roughly 300,000-title catalog and returns curated results with direct "Watch on Tubi" deep links, bypassing all external search navigation, and with ChatGPT commanding an estimated 400 million weekly active users, Tubi secured mass top-of-funnel discovery without JustWatch or Reelgood indexing it. Likewise, a highly capitalized entrant originating from Bill Gates's private office, launched Pix in late 2023 as a "personal entertainment companion," combining OpenAI's language capabilities with a proprietary base of over 600 million consumer taste data points from 6 million registered users; Pix takes conceptual natural-language queries via app, SMS, email, or connected-TV interfaces and proactively pushes notifications when relevant titles land on a user's subscribed platforms. Lumio's "Project Neo" skips the destination app entirely, integrating agentic AI into existing communication infrastructure so a user texts a natural-language command through WhatsApp or Instagram and the agent identifies content and interfaces directly with the smart TV to initiate playback, positioning the conversational software layer, not the hardware maker or the platform, as arbiter of choice. Frenzi, a Mumbai-based startup founded in 2020 and positioned as a "Gen Z-first AI search engine" for OTT discovery across more than 60 services, raised $662K in seed funding led by SucSEED Indovation Fund and pairs multidimensional AI search (mood, theme, social cohort) with built-in social mechanics to keep discovery in a closed loop; the two source passes attribute different founders to Frenzi, Shoma Bose and Deepak Jayaram in one, Balkrishna Hari Singh and Harvinderjit Singh Bhatia in the other, each single-sourced to startup databases, so founder attribution is left open here while the company facts both passes agree on, Mumbai base, 2020 founding, the SucSEED seed amount, 60-plus OTT coverage, and the Gen-Z positioning, are treated as settled. PlayPilot, a Swedish aggregator founded in 2014 with 5.7 million users, has responded to the same shift by decentralizing rather than defending a destination, injecting "PlayLink Cards" and widgets directly into publishers, blogs, and review sites to capture intent where users already read about content. And the platforms are defending themselves in kind: by mid-2025 Netflix was expanding its own generative-AI strategy, testing voice-led natural-language discovery inside its native interface to address choice paralysis and keep the discovery lifecycle inside its walled garden.

Together these confirm that AI-agent-mediated discovery is not prospective, it is underway on multiple fronts simultaneously: LLM-native platform integration, standalone AI companions, messaging-infrastructure agents, AI-first regional entrants, and incumbent platform countermeasures. The survival path repeatedly identified for legacy Funnel vendors is to become backend data licensors to the LLM orchestration layer, supplying ground-truth availability APIs that keep agents from hallucinating, rather than defending centralized consumer destinations.

## Four documented cases of predictive accuracy

Four concrete, sourced cases replace what was once an unsupported assertion that Funnel-layer intent data is a "highly sensitive leading indicator." Following the November 2019 launch of Disney+, Sensor Tower data showed the app generating over 35 million downloads across the App Store and Google Play in Q4 2019, capturing 34% of all premium SVOD app downloads in the period, with an estimated $97.2 million in mobile user spending in the first 60 days, implying Disney captured 16% of all US SVOD revenue that quarter; the velocity data let institutional investors isolate organic demand from subsidized bundling, such as Verizon's free tier, and validate historic subscriber momentum ahead of Disney's Q1 2020 earnings confirmation.

After Netflix guided to a 2 million global subscriber loss for Q2 2022, and with a seemingly strong content slate, *Stranger Things* season 4, fueling debate over whether the guidance was expectation management, Hedgeye analyzed Sensor Tower app-download telemetry through June 2022 and found Netflix tracking toward its worst US download quarter since Q4 2014, while Antenna's Wallet-layer data simultaneously showed domestic churn doubling to 4% with new sign-ups depressed. The combination, a collapsing top of funnel structurally unable to offset elevated churn, accurately telegraphed the loss weeks before the July 19, 2022 earnings report, and stands as the layer's cleanest documented cross-corroboration with Wallet-layer data.

From over 35 million discrete streaming interactions captured January through March 2026 across its user base, JustWatch's intent data, watchlist additions, click-throughs to offers, titles marked seen, showed Netflix (19%) and Prime Video (17%) retaining US SVOD leadership while Disney+ (16%) and Apple TV+ (12%) measurably narrowed the gap, with Apple TV+'s gains correlating to elevated interaction rates on *Shrinking*, *Severance*, and *The F1 Movie*. Published at the close of Q1 2026, ahead of platform subscriber disclosures, this is the first documented case of watchlist intent specifically, rather than download velocity, functioning as a verified leading indicator of share reallocation.

Finally, Sensor Tower's "State of Mobile" usage analytics flagged short-form drama apps growing 5.78 billion hours of time spent year over year globally and generating $2.98 billion in in-app revenue in 2025, and by Q4 2025 its user-level session tracking showed ReelShort users averaging 35.7 minutes per day in the US market against Amazon Prime Video's 26.9, Netflix's 24.8, and Disney+'s 23, independently echoed by Omdia and Marketing Dive coverage. Legacy streamers held total user count while losing daily engagement intensity, a predictive frame for both the streamers' mobile-interface pressure and the sudden multibillion-dollar valuations of micro-drama publishers.
