## 1. Layer mechanics: synthetic demand indices and cross-layer triangulation

Demand/Valuation vendors do not hold authorized platform telemetry or hardware-level ACR. They ingest screen-level consumption signals, global social media activity and sentiment, search engine query velocity, peer-to-peer piracy downloads, and fan wiki engagement, then harmonize those touchpoints into synthetic demand indices that proxy actual viewership. Parrot Analytics claims a correlation above 0.9 between its global demand-expression index and reported subscriber growth across major streaming platforms, which is what lets analysts build synthetic proxies for locked ecosystems such as Apple TV+ and Amazon Prime Video. As SVOD platforms withhold precise viewership, engagement, and subscriber metrics, these indices function as the primary quantitative baseline for carriage licensing, contract renewals, and catalog asset valuation for independent studios, talent agencies, and financial institutions.

The core financial metric is the implied cost-per-demand-point: an estimated (often leaked or reported) production budget for a premium original series divided by its registered global demand-expression footprint. If the resulting figure runs above historical benchmarks, the platform is structurally overpaying for audience acquisition relative to the market; if a client's show delivers an efficient cost-per-demand-point, an agency uses that same figure to demand richer backend participation, higher renewal fees, or co-exclusive licensing terms at renewal.

Institutional users (hedge funds, private equity analysts, studio corporate development teams) run a standard cross-layer triangulation workflow. An analyst first identifies a large unseasonal spike in demand expressions for a title or franchise in this layer. Because demand expressions (social velocity, wiki searches, piracy downloads) tend to precede subscription, the signal is time-lagged by days or weeks and then cross-referenced against bottom-of-funnel transactional data from the Wallet Layer (credit card panel vendors such as YipitData, Consumer Edge, or Antenna). After econometric controls for confounders (localized price increases, promotional discounts, simultaneous competitor releases), the analyst can establish a causal link between a specific piece of IP and platform revenue, reverse-engineering subscriber acquisition costs and marginal title-level revenue contribution without any cooperation from the platform.

The layer as of mid-2026 is being reshaped by two simultaneous forces: private-equity-driven consolidation ending the era of the independent data boutique (Sections 2.1 and 2.4 below), and an existential methodological threat from LLM-mediated discovery, which silos the top-of-funnel intent signal these vendors scrape (Section 2.6).

## 2. Findings by research objective

### 2.1 Ampere/PlumResearch integration

On May 21, 2026, UK-based private equity firm Goldenpeak announced a majority investment in Ampere Analysis, the London-based film/TV/gaming/sports research firm founded in 2015, marking Ampere's first institutional backing since founding. Goldenpeak, led by Mark Williams, deployed the capital from its second fund, launched in 2025. Because the transaction constituted a fundamental change of business for the seller under AIM Rule 15, it required formal shareholder approval: at a General Meeting on June 8, 2026, shareholders representing approximately 44.3% of issued share capital committed support, and the transaction closed June 10, 2026.

Simultaneously, on May 26, 2026, Ampere announced the acquisition of Warsaw-based streaming audience analytics firm PlumResearch for an undisclosed fee, funded by the newly injected Goldenpeak capital. PlumResearch, founded 2015, had scaled its panel-based, device-agnostic tracking across more than 75 international markets, with Tar Heel Capital Pathfinder as its primary first investor (additional early backers Montis Capital and PFR Ventures appear in one pass only; see fault lines). Plum's 20-person core technical team, out of 42 total employees, was integrated into Ampere's corporate structure. Leadership of both firms was retained for continuity: Richard Broughton continues as Executive Director of Ampere Analysis, Dan Stevenson as Managing Director of Ampere Analysis, Lukasz Jeziorski (PlumResearch founder) remains CEO of PlumResearch, and Jonathan Broughton remains PlumResearch's Research Director.

The standalone-versus-absorbed question resolves clearly: Showlabs, Plum's proprietary viewing-log telemetry platform, survived the integration as an active, distinct product line operated by "PlumResearch, an Ampere Analysis company," rather than being sunset or folded into Ampere's backend forecasting suite. On June 16, 2026, NIQ (NielsenIQ, powered by GfK intelligence) commercially launched Showlabs in Japan, delivering cross-platform analysis of viewing trends across Netflix, Prime Video, Disney+, and Crunchyroll, built on anonymized viewing log data modeled against local demographics and platform-share dynamics. The stated buyer base for the Japan deployment: production companies, distributors, broadcasters, and advertisers needing empirical data for overseas expansion and distribution negotiations.
