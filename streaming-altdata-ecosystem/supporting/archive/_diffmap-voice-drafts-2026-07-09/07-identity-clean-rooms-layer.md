## Layer thesis: the toll beneath the tolls

Identity resolution and clean rooms are the substrate under every other layer of this ecosystem. This is the infrastructure that lets Wallet-layer transaction data get matched to Glass-layer viewership data without either party exchanging raw personally identifiable information: PII (emails, phone numbers, name-and-address records) is hashed, salted, or tokenized into persistent identifiers, and matching happens inside a controlled server environment where neither side sees the other's raw records. Whoever owns the match keys and the matching environment collects a toll on every data collaboration that crosses it, which is why the layer's defining 2026 event is an ownership change: the last major independent operator of that toll booth, LiveRamp, agreed in May 2026 to sell itself to Publicis Groupe, and the neutral-intermediary model that the whole open-web data economy was built on is being replaced by holding-company ownership on one side and retail walled gardens (Amazon Marketing Cloud, Walmart Connect) on the other.

The consolidation cycle is nearly complete. LiveRamp itself acquired clean-room pioneer Habu in 2024. WPP acquired InfoSum in April 2025 and embedded it in GroupM. Omnicom completed its acquisition of Interpublic Group in November 2025 (a combined entity with over $25 billion in revenue), folding IPG's Acxiom data asset into Omnicom's proprietary Omni platform. With Publicis taking LiveRamp, the three largest global agency holding companies will wholly own the foundational identity, data-collaboration, and clean-room assets that power the open internet, converting shared utilities into competitive moats.

## 1. LiveRamp/Publicis deal status and client attrition

### Deal terms and mechanics

On May 16, 2026, LiveRamp Holdings, Inc. entered a definitive Agreement and Plan of Merger with MMS USA Holdings, Inc., a wholly owned Publicis Groupe subsidiary, in an all-cash transaction at $38.50 per share, a 29.8% premium over LiveRamp's $29.66 close on May 15, 2026, the last trading day before announcement. Per Publicis Groupe's own press release (publicisgroupe.com, 2026-05-17), the anchor citation for the deal terms: enterprise value $2.167 billion, total equity value $2.546 billion, with $379 million in acquired net cash bridging the two ($2.546 billion minus $379 million equals $2.167 billion exactly).

The DEFM14A merger proxy filed with the SEC details the negotiation endgame: LiveRamp CEO Scott Howe negotiated Publicis up to a "best and final" $38.50 per share, memorialized in a written response on April 26, 2026, countersigned by Publicis on April 30, with board approvals on May 15 and 16 and an Evercore fairness opinion delivered to the LiveRamp board on May 16. Structure per the proxy: newly formed Covey Merger Sub merges into LiveRamp, which survives as a wholly owned subsidiary. Approval requires the affirmative vote of at least 66 2/3% of outstanding shares; LiveRamp had 60,786,315 shares of common stock outstanding as of the record date. A virtual special meeting of stockholders is set for August 17, 2026, and the LiveRamp board unanimously recommended adoption. The agreement carries a $32.35 million break fee under specified termination scenarios (including LiveRamp terminating to accept a superior proposal), "no-shop" restrictions with a narrow fiduciary-out for bona fide written acquisition proposals before shareholder approval, and conversion of outstanding LiveRamp equity awards (options, restricted stock, performance stock units) into restricted cash awards at $38.50 per share on existing vesting schedules, accelerating on qualifying terminations within 24 months of closing.

Financing and integration parameters (single-source, see Fault lines): the transaction has no financing condition. Publicis intends to fund the all-cash consideration from cash on hand plus new corporate bonds issued in the second half of 2026, with modeled peak net financial leverage of roughly 1.2x in fiscal 2027, preserving its BBB+ and Baa1 investment-grade ratings. LiveRamp lands in Publicis's Technology reporting segment (roughly 14% of net revenue, housing Publicis Sapient, Epsilon, and the Marcel AI operating system), distinct from the Epsilon reporting line, operating as an independent business with Scott Howe continuing as CEO reporting directly to Publicis CEO Arthur Sadoun. On a calendarized 2026 consensus, LiveRamp is projected at roughly $126 million of non-GAAP EBITDA (including an $80 million share-based-compensation adjustment to Publicis accounting standards), with $50 million in anticipated pre-tax run-rate savings making the deal accretive to headline EPS in year one. On the strength of the deal, Publicis raised its constant-currency targets for fiscal 2027 and 2028: net revenue growth to +7% to +8% (from +6% to +7%) and headline EPS growth to +8% to +10% (from +7% to +9%).

### Regulatory posture and status

The transaction remains pending, not closed, and is advancing through its gating items. Preliminary proxy materials (Schedule 14A) went to employees June 2, 2026. Publicis and LiveRamp filed HSR premerger notifications with the DOJ and FTC on June 11, 2026. Closing is subject to HSR clearance, CFIUS review, and non-US antitrust and foreign direct investment approvals. The outside date is May 16, 2027, with a three-month extension mechanism, though both parties publicly target closing before the end of calendar 2026 (Run 02's table states a December 31, 2026 target close date). Shareholder rights firm Johnson Fistel, PLLP opened a customary investigation in June 2026 into whether the LiveRamp board ran a fair process; it has not affected the timeline. Deal status was separately re-confirmed 2026-07-07 against Publicis Groupe's own press release: signed 2026-05-17, still pending regulatory approval and the shareholder vote, no reported change in terms, competing bid, or regulatory objection as of that check (see Gaps remaining for the caveat on the source passes themselves).

### Strategic logic: the capstone of a vertical-integration program

The acquisition completes a deliberate multi-year Publicis buildout from advertising agency into vertically integrated marketing-technology enterprise: Epsilon in 2019 ($4.4 billion, deterministic CRM identity, 2.3 billion consumer profiles), CitrusAd in 2021 (retail media technology), Profitero in 2022 (commerce analytics), and Lotame in March 2025 (1.6 billion global identifiers added to the Epsilon stack). LiveRamp supplies the missing piece: open-web identity infrastructure and the clean-room platform connecting brands to more than 25,000 publisher domains and over 500 technology partners. Publicis frames the combination as "data co-creation," secure cross-partner dataset enrichment used to train bespoke enterprise AI agents, synthesizing Epsilon's deterministic identity assets with LiveRamp's multi-market collaboration platform so that co-created datasets, rather than static public large language models, become the training foundation for client-specific agents.
