## 1. IAS post-privatization (Novacap)

**Deal mechanics.** Novacap, a North American private equity firm with over $10 billion in assets under management, closed its all-cash take-private of Integral Ad Science on December 23, 2025, at approximately $1.9 billion enterprise value (equity value frequently cited at $1.6 billion), paying $10.30 per share, a 22% premium over the September 23, 2025 close, the last full trading day before announcement (PR Newswire deal announcements; SEC preliminary information statement). The transaction ran through Novacap's TMT VI fund via the acquisition entities Igloo Group Parent, Inc. and its Merger Sub, capitalized with up to $857.8 million in equity commitments plus a $1.0 billion term loan and a $150 million revolving credit facility under a new Credit Agreement with Royal Bank of Canada; IAS's prior credit facilities were terminated at close (SEC filings). IAS stock was halted on Nasdaq before the December 23 open, suspended effective December 24, and the company delisted and deregistered from SEC reporting. The deal completed Vista Equity Partners' exit: Vista had taken a majority stake in 2018, steered the 2021 IPO, and held over 65 million shares at close, all converted to the $10.30 cash consideration. Advisors: Jefferies and Kirkland & Ellis for IAS; Evercore and Willkie Farr & Gallagher for Novacap.

**Executive restructuring.** The decisive post-close signal came July 7, 2026: Lidiane Jones was appointed CEO, succeeding Lisa Utzschneider, who had led IAS for over seven years (since 2019; formerly Yahoo CRO and Amazon VP) and moves to Special Advisor to the IAS board through end of 2026 while also advising Novacap across its portfolio (IAS corporate announcement). Jones is an enterprise-software and AI technologist, not an ad-tech operator: CEO of Bumble immediately prior, CEO of Slack under Salesforce before that, EVP and General Manager of Digital Experiences at Salesforce, and over a decade of product leadership at Microsoft and Sonos. The read: IAS is repositioning media quality verification away from a commoditized, volume-based data feed and toward enterprise-grade AI software, with a mandate to accelerate walled-garden integration and rebuild the fraud and contextual engines as AI-native.

The Jones appointment capped a C-suite rebuild that Novacap had already run through late 2024 and 2025 (IAS corporate announcements and SEC filings): Alpana Wegner as CFO effective June 10, 2025 (formerly CFO of Secureworks and Benefitfocus, with M&A and business-transformation experience, including the sale of Secureworks); Marc Grabowski as COO on September 16, 2024 (formerly Global VP of Oracle Advertising, recruited after Oracle's ad-tech retreat, with legacy Moat/Grapeshot experience); and Srishti Gupta as CPO the same day (formerly CPO at Rokt), focused on CTV and retail media. Utzschneider's continuity through the transition was secured with a $4.0 million service-vested retention bonus. With public trading ceased, unvested RSUs were converted into contingent cash awards on their original vesting schedules, shifting incentives away from public-equity upside.

**Commercial repositioning and liquidity path.** Post-close, IAS is restructuring commercial operations and investing in programmatic expertise to drive growth in Private Marketplaces while maintaining direct publisher relationships, positioning its verification capabilities closer to the premium transaction layer as buyers retreat from open exchanges (trade press). The privatization rationale is structural: operating a Shield-layer platform means processing trillions of transactions in milliseconds, and the AI infrastructure spend that requires compresses margins in ways public markets punish quarter to quarter; private ownership buys time to rebuild for a cookieless, privacy-restricted environment. Novacap's adjacent holdings sharpen the thesis: it acquired convergent-TV specialist Cadent for $600 million in 2023 and backed Cadent's $324 million acquisition of machine-learning ad-tech firm AdTheorent in 2024, creating at least the hypothetical option of a vertically integrated buy-plus-verify stack. On liquidity: private equity hold periods of three to five years, plus the priority of servicing the $1.0 billion term loan, make a re-IPO or sale highly unlikely before 2028 or 2029. If the AI integration lifts gross margin and recurring-revenue predictability under Jones, the more probable exit is a strategic sale to a large enterprise software acquirer (the Salesforce/Adobe/Oracle class) seeking native MRC-accredited verification, toward the end of the decade.
