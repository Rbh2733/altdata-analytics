## Findings by research objective

### 1. Amazon's dual posture, quantified

Amazon operates the most heavily monetized dual posture in the ecosystem: it withholds granular viewership telemetry from the studios and licensors supplying Prime Video, while selling deterministic audience targeting built on that same viewing behavior to advertisers.

**The withholding side.** Amazon's FY2025 consolidated net sales reached $716.9 billion, up 12% from $638.0 billion in 2024, with operating income of $80.0 billion (up from $68.6 billion), per Amazon's Q4 2025 earnings release and 2025 annual report filed with the SEC. Prime Video's standalone economics are never broken out. They sit inside the "Subscription Services" line ($49.62 billion in FY2025, up 11.8% from $44.37 billion), which bundles Prime memberships, digital video, audiobooks, digital music, and e-books. Because a Prime membership is accounted for as a "single stand-ready obligation," studios licensing content to Prime Video cannot isolate the subscriber-acquisition or retention value of their own intellectual property from public disclosures. AWS, for scale context, did $128.7 billion in FY2025 (up 19.6%) with $45.6 billion of operating income, underscoring that streaming is a small piece of a much larger machine.

**The monetization side.** Advertising Services is Amazon's fastest-growing major segment: $56.0 billion in FY2024, crossing a $70 billion trailing-twelve-month run rate by Q1 2026 (Q1 2026 advertising services revenue of $17.24 billion, up 24% year over year, per Amazon's Q1 2026 earnings release, aboutamazon.com, April 29, 2026). Analyst estimates put advertising gross margin above 90% with operating margins near 50%, because the ad stack layers directly onto existing retail, traffic, and customer data.

**Amazon Marketing Cloud mechanics.** AMC is the operational instrument of the dual posture: a privacy-safe clean room built natively on AWS, in which advertisers run aggregate SQL queries across Amazon's deterministic first-party signals (Prime Video ad exposures, search clicks, retail purchases) joined against their own first-party data (CRM logs, loyalty metrics), with outputs delivered only as aggregated metrics and no row-level PII exposed. AMC lets advertisers compute exact incremental return on ad spend and run multi-touch attribution linking a top-funnel Prime Video impression to a bottom-funnel marketplace purchase. The clean room itself is free for eligible advertisers, but access requires executing media spend through the Amazon DSP: withheld viewership data functions as a toll booth into Amazon's ad stack. Recent expansions deepen the asymmetry: the ad-traffic lookback window was extended from 13 to 25 months following unBoxed 2025, and separately Amazon unlocked up to 5 years of historical data for retail purchase and video viewership signals (per Amazon's own advertising.amazon.com announcement; see Fault lines for how these two figures were initially confused). As of June 2, 2026, the Amazon Retail Purchases and Flexible Shopping Insights datasets are available to all AMC users at no additional cost, letting brands track customer lifetime value and organic shopping behavior independent of ad exposure. A commissioned Forrester Consulting Total Economic Impact study published February 2026 credits AMC plus the Amazon DSP with 240% ROI, up to 65% reduction in wasted ad spend, and 30% to 50% higher return on ad spend.

**The on-record complaint.** The asymmetry is formally contested. The Writers Guild of America has filed unfair-labor-practice charges with the National Labor Relations Board against Amazon, explicitly citing the withholding of Prime Video subscriber numbers as a tactic to underpay writers whose residual compensation is tied to subscriber tiers (the current labor agreements set a viewership-based residual threshold of 20% of a platform's domestic subscriber base, a figure creators cannot verify without third-party data). This NLRB charge is a stronger, more current citation than the earlier WGA and Strategic Organizing Center letters to the FTC about the MGM acquisition, which made the same anticompetitive-withholding argument. Amazon's Prime Video legal agreements meanwhile explicitly codify its right to withhold data and offset payments, structurally institutionalizing supplier blindness. ### 2. Apple's posture, resolved

Apple does not run a dual posture. It runs an isolationist one, and the question this layer was asked to resolve (AMC-equivalent clean room, or pure ecosystem bait?) resolves cleanly to ecosystem bait.

Apple's opacity exceeds Amazon's. FY2025 (ended September 27, 2025) total net sales were $416.16 billion, up 6%, per Apple's 10-K. The entire software, subscription, and streaming ecosystem is condensed into one "Services" segment: $109.15 billion in FY2025, up 13% from $96.16 billion, at a 75.4% gross margin (versus 36.8% gross margin for Products). There is zero breakout of Apple TV+ subscribers, churn, or ARPU, which is precisely why the Wallet layer (Antenna, Consumer Edge panels) exists as the only source of synthetic sign-up and cancellation proxies for the service.

On the clean-room question: Apple operates no programmatic data-monetization clean room comparable to Amazon Marketing Cloud or Roku Data Cloud. Its advertising revenue comes primarily from App Store search ads, not from selling streaming audience segments. In November 2025, Apple's head of Services, Eddy Cue, confirmed on the record that the company has "no plans at this time" to introduce an ad-supported tier for Apple TV+ (per press coverage of the statement). Apple TV+ is a high-budget, non-monetized loss leader whose job is hardware retention (iPhone net sales were $209.58 billion in FY2025) and Apple One bundle survival. The strategic consequence: because Apple has no second-order data business to protect, its incentives run entirely toward hardware-level privacy enforcement, and it wields the App Tracking Transparency framework as a weapon that deprecates third-party identifiers across the broader ad-tech ecosystem while building no replacement infrastructure for external buyers. Apple is structurally different from the other Variant A gatekeepers, not a smaller version of the same thing. ### 3. Roku's centrality

Before the June 2026 Fox acquisition, Roku was the purest Variant A gatekeeper: unlike Amazon and Apple, whose streaming postures are side effects of much larger businesses, data withholding and monetization was Roku's central, existential business model.

Roku's FY2025 total net revenue was $4.74 billion, up 15%, with a return to net income of $88.4 million, per Roku's Q4/FY2025 shareholder letter. In early 2026 Roku recast its reporting from a single "Platform" line into three segments: Advertising ($2.33 billion, up 13%, driven by 68% higher video ad impressions despite pricing pressure), Subscriptions ($1.82 billion, up 25%, supported by owned-and-operated services Frndly TV and Howdy), and Devices ($592 million, flat, managed near break-even). Advertising plus Subscriptions (the former Platform segment) generated $4.14 billion, over 87% of total revenue; hardware is effectively a loss leader run to acquire viewership telemetry, generating 145.6 billion streaming hours across roughly 90 million global streaming households in 2025. The Q1 2026 quarterly cut tells the same story: total revenue $1.25 billion with platform revenue up 28% to $1.13 billion (Advertising $613 million, up 27%; Subscriptions $519 million, up 30%), net income of $86 million, and record trailing-twelve-month free cash flow of $538.8 million, per the Q1 2026 shareholder letter.

To monetize the telemetry, Roku retired its legacy OneView ad-stack strategy (which functioned more as an exclusive ad-tech mandate than an open ecosystem) in favor of the Roku Data Cloud, a clean-room environment letting partners analyze Roku's proprietary TV data for programmatic measurement, plus the self-serve Roku Ads Manager aimed at small and medium businesses. The Roku Channel gives Roku a large owned-and-operated FAST inventory position that it monetizes without paying upfront content licensing costs (its exact viewing share is an open fault line, below). No disclosure isolates Roku Data Cloud or OneView revenue specifically; it stays bundled inside Platform/Advertising (see Gaps remaining).

The incentive implication versus Amazon and Apple: because platform monetization is Roku's whole business rather than a rounding error, Roku negotiates with the Wallet, Glass, and Identity/Clean Rooms layers as a principal defending its core asset, not as a diversified giant defending a side business. Its absolute reliance on ad monetization also made it uniquely exposed to consolidation pressure and competition for ad dollars from Amazon and Netflix, which is the vulnerability the Fox deal resolved.
