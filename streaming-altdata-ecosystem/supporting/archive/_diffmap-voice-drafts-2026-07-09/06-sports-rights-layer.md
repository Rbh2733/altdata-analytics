## Positioning within the framework (taxonomy pull, Run B)

The sports-rights layer sits inside the eight-layer alt-data architecture as the sub-vertical handling what standard SVOD transaction panels cannot: synchronous low-latency delivery, betting-handle correlation, and regional blackout compliance at scale. Demand for the whole ecosystem is generated structurally by Layer 0 (Amazon, Apple, Roku, Google/YouTube, Disney, Fox) withholding downstream metrics and monetizing withheld first-party data through Roku Data Cloud and Amazon Marketing Cloud, which forces studios and investors to build synthetic proxies through the Wallet (Antenna, YipitData, Consumer Edge), Glass (Inscape, Samba TV, VideoAmp, iSpot, Nielsen), Shield (DoubleVerify, IAS), Demand/Valuation (Parrot Analytics, PlumResearch, Luminate), Funnel (JustWatch, Reelgood, Sensor Tower), and Identity (LiveRamp, UID2, AMC) layers. Cross-layer triangulation is the operating method: a Parrot demand spike checked against Antenna transaction cohorts, controlled for price changes, resolved through clean-room identity matching. Layer 6 is structurally unique within this stack because it runs on exclusive, multi-year league data licensing rather than consumer transaction consent, which is why its vendors (Stats Perform, Sportradar, Genius Sports) hold pricing power no panel vendor has.

The operative thesis, carried over from the Great Collision framing and validated across all three passes: exclusive league data rights function as a permission-layer toll, structurally parallel to semiconductor lithography access. Official data must be licensed by every participant in the downstream ecosystem (competing networks, regulated sportsbooks, programmatic advertisers, and now prediction markets) regardless of which bidder wins the video rights. As broadcast rights costs escalate, the data layer is consolidating into fewer, deeper, longer deals, increasingly laced with direct league equity in the vendors, not fragmenting.

## Findings by research objective

### 1. Stats Perform's FIFA execution

In January 2026 FIFA appointed Stats Perform as its first-ever official worldwide betting-data and betting-streaming rights distributor (inside.fifa.com and statsperform.com releases, January 2026; SportsPro coverage). The multi-year agreement grants exclusive distribution of live data and video for the expanded 48-team, 104-match 2026 Men's World Cup across the United States, Canada, and Mexico, the 2027 Women's World Cup in Brazil, the 2028 Futsal World Cup, the 2026 and 2028 U-20 Women's World Cups, the 2027 and 2029 U-20 World Cups, and the FIFA Intercontinental Cup through 2029, plus exclusive betting rights to thousands of member-association matches per season powered by the FIFA+ platform.

Execution rests on a dual-engine architecture. RunningBall handles ultra-low latency in-play data: trained scouts inside stadiums on dedicated secure Wi-Fi deliver deterministic pitch-to-trading-floor data in under one second, the requirement for automated market making, longer live betting windows, avoidance of unnecessary market suspensions, and immediate settlement. Opta handles the semantic and predictive tier: player statistics, match trackers, and player-prop feeds, running proprietary foundation models trained on 7.2 petabytes of historical sports data with direct broadcast-video ingestion. Opta's Expected Goals on Target (xGOT) model is the flagship for 2026: unlike pre-shot xG, it is a post-shot model evaluating goal likelihood from the actual shot trajectory and end location in the goalmouth, isolating finishing skill and goalkeeper performance, which lets sportsbooks price highly specific in-play markets (statsperform.com xGOT insights). The Bet LiveStreams service additionally distributes live match video directly into licensed sportsbook applications in selected territories, bundling data with video so the sportsbook becomes a sticky media surface rather than a spreadsheet, with distribution integration extending to TikTok and DAZN.

The issues-and-challenges half of the objective: the 104-match format has radically expanded the corruption surface. The core structural risk is micro-bet spot-fixing, since RunningBall enables wagers on isolated events (exact minute of a throw-in, specific player cards, corner counts) that a single player can manipulate without throwing the match, disguised as ordinary error. Critics frame this as a paradox: commercializing ultra-fast data through Stats Perform deepens the sport's reliance on the betting ecosystem and multiplies the markets requiring surveillance. FIFA's response was an Integrity Task Force established in May 2026, integrating Stats Perform's integrity division alongside the FBI, INTERPOL, the UN Office on Drugs and Crime, and the integrity units of competitors Sportradar and Genius Sports, using market monitoring and reporting tools embedded in the official FIFA Player app. As of the research date (July 2026, mid-tournament) Stats Perform retained exclusivity with no reported commercial breaches or competitive challenges to its position.

### 2. Sports Innovation Lab/Zoomph, one year on

The April 2025 partnership (PRWeb and Zoomph announcements) fused SIL's deterministic Fluid Fan Graph, built on observational and transactional data (billions of indexed purchases, ticket sales, merchandise, and subscription transactions across millions of households) with Zoomph's real-time AI-driven social audience intelligence, targeting sponsorship measurement across social, broadcast, and digital.

What actually shipped, with named clients (sportsilab.com case material, late 2025): fan-segmentation work for the Washington Wizards identifying high-value audiences to optimize ad spend and lift ticket sales; market-level data analysis across all 12 WNBA teams powering the league's five-year growth and fan-acquisition strategy; and quantification of the impact of Ally Financial's 50/50 pledge on gender-equity sports spending. Broader named users of the SIL stack: Legends, Kellanova, NASCAR, and Monumental Sports and Entertainment. Reported aggregate results for brands using SIL-targeted audiences: 4% higher engagement, 16% lower cost per acquisition, and a 0.2x return-on-ad-spend lift. SIL's underlying research claim: Fluid Fans are roughly 10% of the sports fan base but drive more than 50% of total revenue, with women's-sports fans the pinnacle archetype (early adopters of streaming and direct commerce out of necessity), rewarding early sponsors such as Budweiser (NWSL), Visa (USWNT), and Nike (WNBA, NWSL, USWNT).

The partnership's endgame was acquisition: Genius Sports bought SIL in September 2025 for an undisclosed sum (investors.geniussports.com release). The strategic logic is closed-loop attribution: Genius's FanHub ID already tracked media consumption across digital, social, mobile, and CTV but lacked the link to actual commerce; the Fluid Fan Graph supplied the transactional half. The combination, productized through Genius's FANHub programmatic offering and its Moment Engine advertising product, lets brands connect campaign exposure during live games to measurable real-world transactions. Financial impact was immediate: Genius's media division reported an 88.9% year-over-year revenue jump to $41.8 million in Q3 2025, credited to the SIL acquisition and the FANHub rollout (Genius Q3 2025 earnings).

### 3. Streaming platforms' in-house sports-data ambitions

The answer across passes is a strategic bifurcation, not a binary build-versus-buy. For advertising monetization, audience targeting, personalization, and delivery infrastructure, the platforms are absolutely building in-house, with individual technology budgets of $1 billion to over $5 billion annually and hundreds of dedicated AI scientists. For certified live match integrity, betting settlement, and official optical tracking, they remain wholly dependent on the vendor triopoly, because replicating sub-second certified collection across hundreds of federations plus regulated sportsbook API settlement is prohibitive even at Big Tech scale.
