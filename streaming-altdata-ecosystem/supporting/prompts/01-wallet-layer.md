# Deep Research Prompt — Wallet Layer (Subscriber Transaction Intelligence)

> Feed this file directly into Gemini Deep Research or Google Finance Deep Research. It is a prompt, not a report. Do not pre-read `streaming-altdata-ecosystem.md` into the research run beyond the context below, the point is to go past what that map already holds.

## Context (orientation only, do not treat as exhaustive)

The streaming alt-data ecosystem exists because platforms withhold subscriber, viewership, and ad economics that studios, advertisers, and investors need. The Wallet layer is the ground-truth layer within that ecosystem: it isolates the actual billing relationship using credit/debit card streams, bank API feeds, and digital invoice tracking, and from that derives cohort survival curves, plan-mix migration (premium to ad-supported), cross-platform wallet mapping, and serial-churner behavior over 12-month windows. Known players as of mid-2026: YipitData (broad multi-sector incumbent, Carlyle-backed, absorbed 1010data in 2023), Consumer Edge (acquired Earnest Analytics April 2025), Antenna (streaming-specific specialist, BDMI/Raine Ventures-backed), Bloomberg Second Measure, M Science. This layer's distinguishing legal exposure is financial-transaction consent, not device consent, which puts it directly in the path of CFPB Section 1033 and California's Delete Act (DROP).

## Research objective

Go deeper than vendor identity and one-time funding events. Establish current competitive position, unit economics under regulatory pressure, and where consolidation is headed next.

1. **Current ownership and capital structure.** For YipitData, Consumer Edge, Antenna, Bloomberg Second Measure, and M Science: confirm current ownership/investor cap tables, any funding, debt, or IPO-track developments since mid-2026, and whether YipitData's rumored 2026-2027 IPO or exit process has advanced, stalled, or been confirmed/denied.
2. **Margin compression from the JPMorgan/Plaid precedent.** Has the per-pull API-access-fee model spread beyond JPMorgan to other tier-1 banks (Bank of America, Wells Fargo, Citi)? What are Wallet-layer vendors (not just aggregators like Plaid) actually paying now, and is there public commentary from YipitData, Consumer Edge, or Antenna on how this is affecting their pricing to end clients?
3. **DROP compliance reality, not just the statute.** Has an actual Wallet-layer vendor been named in a DROP enforcement action, fine, or public compliance failure since the August 1, 2026 deadline? What is the realistic operational cost these vendors are reporting or being estimated at for ongoing 45-day query cycles?
4. **Antenna's specific product depth.** What does Antenna's "Subscriber Views" product (ACR-plus-transaction fusion) actually cost, who has publicly adopted it, and does it face direct competition from a similar fused product at YipitData or Consumer Edge?
5. **Consumer Edge/Earnest Analytics integration progress.** One year-plus post-acquisition, has the combined entity shipped a genuinely fused product (transaction plus healthcare/pharmacy claims), or do the two data assets still operate as separate practice areas under one parent?
6. **New entrants or exits.** Has any new Wallet-layer competitor emerged (particularly one built on zero-party/incentivized-panel architecture, immune to DROP/VPPA exposure by design) or has any existing player exited/shut down/been acquired since mid-2026?
