## Context (orientation only, do not treat as exhaustive)

This layer scrapes screen-level consumption signals, social/search demand, piracy downloads, and wiki activity to build synthetic demand indices used in IP valuation and licensing negotiation. Key players: Parrot Analytics (cost-per-demand-point metric, >0.9 correlation claimed between demand-expression and subscriber growth), Diesel Labs, Whip Media/TV Time, Luminate, Gracenote (Nielsen subsidiary, supplies metadata ontology rather than competing on demand directly), and PlumResearch (acquired by Ampere Analysis in May 2026, backed by Goldenpeak). The standing case study is NBCUniversal's licensing of *Suits* to Netflix: $356 million in measured UCAN subscriber revenue Q1 2020-Q4 2024 per Parrot's Streaming Economics model, over $800 million globally since 2020, with NBCU's strategic withholding of the final season until July 2024 to drive a "halo effect" migration back to Peacock.

## Research objective

1. **Ampere/PlumResearch integration.** What has actually shipped from the combined Ampere-PlumResearch entity since May 2026? Is Plum's device-agnostic tracking still sold as a standalone product or fully absorbed into Ampere's forecasting suite? Any client-facing pricing or positioning changes?
2. **Parrot Analytics' next case study.** *Suits* is now a dated (2020-2024) example. What is Parrot's most recent, comparably-documented licensing/demand case study since mid-2026, ideally with a similarly specific dollar figure and dated methodology note?
3. **Gracenote's actual competitive posture.** Prior passes describe Gracenote as metadata-only rather than a demand competitor. Confirm whether this is still accurate or whether Nielsen has repositioned Gracenote to compete more directly with Parrot Analytics/Ampere on demand-side analytics.
4. **Whip Media/TV Time and Luminate's current relevance.** Both are named but under-detailed in prior research. What is each vendor's current scale, ownership, and primary institutional buyer base?
5. **Talent-agency adoption, concretely.** CAA and WME are cited as using demand-expression data in packaging negotiations. Is there a specific, named, recent (post-2025) deal or dispute where this data was reported to have directly influenced a backend compensation outcome?
6. **New entrants exploiting foundation-model-native demand signals.** Is any vendor building demand indices directly from LLM-mediated search/discovery behavior (as opposed to traditional social/search scraping), given the broader AI-native disruption thread already flagged in the main map?
