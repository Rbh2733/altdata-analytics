## Context (orientation only, do not treat as exhaustive)

This is the layer beneath all the others: identity resolution and clean-room infrastructure lets Wallet-layer transaction data get matched to Glass-layer viewership data without either party exchanging raw PII. LiveRamp (RampID) is the primary independent toll booth, reporting a 99.5% match rate on full PII in an independent Q1 2026 Comscore CustomIQ analysis. The Trade Desk's Unified ID 2.0 (UID2) is the leading open-source alternative. Walled-garden retail clean rooms — Amazon Marketing Cloud and Walmart Connect (powered by Luminate) — force advertisers into the retailer's own server environment; Walmart Connect's ad revenue surpassed $4.8 billion in 2025, up 30% year over year. The single biggest development in this layer: Publicis Groupe agreed to acquire LiveRamp in May 2026 for $38.50/share (a 29.8% premium), $2.167 billion enterprise value, $2.546 billion total equity value, expected to close by year-end 2026. This was independently verified but did not appear in either primary Deep Research pass that built the rest of this ecosystem map — treat it as the freshest, least-cross-checked fact in the whole taxonomy.

## Research objective

1. **LiveRamp/Publicis deal status, current as of your research date.** Has the deal closed, is it still pending regulatory/shareholder approval, or has anything changed (revised terms, competing bid, regulatory objection)? What does Publicis plan to do with LiveRamp's existing agency-agnostic, multi-holding-company client base once it's owned by a single holding company — is there public commentary on client attrition risk from competing agencies (WPP, Omnicom, IPG) who currently use LiveRamp specifically because it's independent?
2. **UID2's adoption trajectory since the Publicis news.** Does the LiveRamp acquisition change UID2's competitive position as "the independent alternative," given LiveRamp itself is about to lose its independence? Any stated reaction from The Trade Desk?
3. **Walled-garden clean room economics, more precisely.** Confirm Amazon Marketing Cloud's actual reported revenue or usage-growth figures if disclosed (prior research only quantifies Walmart Connect, not AMC directly). Is AMC growing faster or slower than Walmart Connect, and is either raising prices or bundling in ways that squeeze independent Wallet/Glass-layer vendors trying to operate through them?
4. **New entrants or protocol shifts.** Is there a credible successor identity framework emerging (post-cookie, post-UID2) being discussed industry-wide, particularly anything tied to agentic/AI-mediated commerce authentication?
5. **Comscore CustomIQ's role.** Comscore ran the independent match-rate analysis validating LiveRamp's 99.5% figure. Is Comscore positioning CustomIQ as a standalone identity-adjacent product line, and does this relate to Comscore's broader 2025-2026 recapitalization and AI-measurement pivot (Stuart Frankel board appointment)?
