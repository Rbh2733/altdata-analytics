# Deep Research Prompt — Glass Layer (Hardware / Automatic Content Recognition)

> Feed this file directly into Gemini Deep Research or Google Finance Deep Research. It is a prompt, not a report. Do not pre-read `streaming-altdata-ecosystem.md` into the research run beyond the context below, the point is to go past what that map already holds.

## Context (orientation only, do not treat as exhaustive)

The Glass layer relies on chipset-level Automatic Content Recognition embedded in smart TVs and set-top boxes (Samba TV, Vizio's Inscape, VideoAmp, iSpot.tv, Nielsen, Comscore) to determine exact screen-level viewership. Its legal exposure is device-level opt-in consent, a different regime from the Wallet layer's financial-transaction consent. Buyers skew toward ad agencies, brand advertisers, and network ad-sales teams trying to establish a transactable TV-advertising currency. Nielsen still holds an estimated 80-90% of national ad-currency share but is mid-crisis: MRC suspended its accreditation in 2021 (pandemic-era undercounting), reaccredited it in 2023, and it faced a renewed credibility episode through 2025-2026, with two materially different tellings of that episode already on file (one centers a VAB variance report and a suppressed "Gauge" report scandal, the other centers MRC-mandated fixes delayed to August 2026) that have not yet been reconciled against primary sources. VideoAmp and iSpot.tv are the leading JIC-certified challengers.

A narrow sub-niche, Attention Verification (EDO Inc., TVision — confirming a human is actually watching versus the set merely being powered on), rides on top of this layer's infrastructure and is included in scope below rather than broken into its own file.

## Research objective

1. **Resolve the Nielsen fault line with primary sources.** Pull the actual MRC and VAB releases (not secondary summaries) covering the 2025-2026 episode. Does Nielsen's accreditation status, as of your research date, reflect the "reaccredited July 2026 with 2025 audit open" framing, the "Gauge suppression scandal" framing, both, or neither? What is the current, dated, ground truth?
2. **VideoAmp and iSpot.tv's actual 2026 upfront share.** Both were reported mid-2026 as scaling rapidly ($3B 2024 upfront volume for VideoAmp, projections near $6B for 2026). Confirm actual 2026 upfront results if disclosed, and whether either has been granted full MRC accreditation (not just JIC certification) since mid-2026.
3. **Samba TV and Vizio Inscape's current standing.** Neither appears prominently in the two prior research passes' Glass-layer competitive section beyond being named. What is Samba TV's current scale, ownership, and any 2026 developments? Note Vizio was acquired by Walmart in 2024 — confirm Inscape's current ownership and whether it still operates as an arm's-length ACR data product or has been absorbed into Walmart Connect's retail-media stack.
4. **Attention Verification niche.** What is the current status of the EDO Inc. v. TVision patent litigation referenced in prior passes? Has either company been acquired, or gained a materially larger footprint, since mid-2026?
5. **Currency-war endgame signal.** Has any major advertiser or agency holding company (WPP, Omnicom, Publicis, IPG) publicly committed to a multi-currency or non-Nielsen-primary measurement standard for the 2027 upfronts?
