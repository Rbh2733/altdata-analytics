# Deep Research Prompt — Shield Layer (Media Quality Verification / Fraud)

> Feed this file directly into Gemini Deep Research or Google Finance Deep Research. It is a prompt, not a report. Do not pre-read `streaming-altdata-ecosystem.md` into the research run beyond the context below, the point is to go past what that map already holds.

## Context (orientation only, do not treat as exhaustive)

The Shield layer verifies that a programmatic impression was delivered to a real, brand-safe, viewable placement, not whether anyone watched it with interest — that distinction belongs to the Demand/Valuation layer. DoubleVerify (NYSE: DV) and Integral Ad Science formed the effective duopoly here until Novacap took IAS private in December 2025 for roughly $1.9 billion, leaving DV as the sole public proxy. DV's May 2026 Global Insights report documented a 140% year-over-year surge in CTV fraud in Q1 2026, including direct-deal (not just open-exchange) campaigns running 25-34% bot traffic in specific case studies, against under 1% fraud in DV-protected inventory versus roughly 9% unprotected. DV's Q1 2026 actuals: $180.8 million revenue (+10% YoY), activation $100.5 million (+6%), measurement $61.8 million (+16%), supply-side $18.5 million (+12%), adjusted EBITDA $55.2 million (31% margin), Q2 2026 guidance of $199-205 million revenue.

## Research objective

1. **IAS post-privatization.** What has Novacap actually done with Integral Ad Science operationally since the December 2025 take-private (pricing changes, product repositioning, executive changes, any signal of a future re-IPO or strategic sale)? Private-equity-owned competitors are often more visible in trade press than in filings, use that channel.
2. **DoubleVerify's actual FY2026 guidance and delivery.** Confirm DV's full-year 2026 revenue and adjusted EBITDA margin guidance as most recently reaffirmed or revised, and how Q2/Q3 2026 actuals (if reported by your research date) compare.
3. **New entrants or disintermediation risk.** Has a scaled competitor entered the Shield layer since the IAS privatization (the Reddit thread referenced in prior research citing "Scope3" reacting to the IAS deal is a lead worth chasing directly), and is there credible evidence any hyperscaler or DSP is building comparable in-house fraud detection that could disintermediate third-party verification entirely?
4. **AI-driven fraud sophistication, concretely.** DV's report cites AI tools enabling a 10x scaling of fraudulent CTV app volume versus 2024. What specific fraud techniques (not just the aggregate stat) are DV, IAS, or independent security researchers describing as the current state of the art, and is there a credible technical countermeasure vendors are converging on?
5. **Advertiser adoption gap.** Prior research cites under 21% of advertisers using invalid-traffic detection as a core KPI despite the fraud data. Is there more recent survey or trade data (IAB, ANA) on whether that adoption rate is moving, and what's the stated reason advertisers give for not prioritizing it?

## Execution constraints

- Prioritize SEC filings (DV's 10-Q/10-K, investor letters), DoubleVerify's own Global Insights report methodology notes, and named trade press over aggregator summaries.
- Every figure needs a source and a date.
- Do not editorialize on investment implications.
